3. CONCERNING PRIOR ART - BRIEF 1 (gating-cell sync engine)
-----------------------------------------------------------

3.1  WO1994028539A2 - Coda Music "Intelligent Accompaniment"
     (Vivace / SmartMusic family).  *Most concerning patent.*

     Derives tempo from the time delta between a matched performer
     event and the corresponding expected-score event.  This is
     direct overlap on the bare "tempo from performer keystroke
     timing" idea.

     Distinction I am drawing:  Vivace is a SCORE-FOLLOWER over a
     continuous performance score with tolerance windows and skip
     thresholds.  It has no authored gating-cell column, no
     composer-marked halt-and-resume points, and no equivalent of
     the NOISE absorption marker.

     What I want from you:  please pull the full Coda / Vivace /
     SmartMusic patent family (continuations, divisionals, foreign
     filings) and compare element-by-element against Section 7 of
     Brief 1.  If the distinction I am drawing does not hold, I
     need to know that before filing.

3.2  B-Keeper - Robertson & Plumbley, Queen Mary University of
     London (2007 NIME paper; extended in Computer Music Journal
     vol. 37 issue 2, 2013).  *Closest academic prior art.*

     A real-time beat tracker that re-tempos a sequencer from a
     live drummer's kick-drum onsets.  No patent (academic
     publication) but on-point for the bare "sync a sequencer to
     a live performer" goal.

     Distinction I am drawing:  B-Keeper is a continuous beat
     tracker over kick onsets, effectively a clock follower.  No
     authored cue rows, no halt/resume, no noise filter.

     What I want from you:  include in the literature search.
     Confirm whether any of its successors or related QMUL Centre
     for Digital Music outputs add gating-cell or halt-resume
     semantics.

3.3  Antescofo (IRCAM) and the broader score-following research
     literature (Cont, Raphael "Music Plus One", Dannenberg).

     Distinct mechanism (operates on continuous audio with HMM
     alignment, not on per-row MIDI cues).  Acknowledged in the
     brief.  Standard search target.

3.4  Conductor / gesture-tempo patents (US20130032023A1,
     US5275082).

     Distinct (no grid, no cues).  Listed for completeness.

3.5  Tap-tempo and audio-BPM-detection patents (JP2780475B2,
     US6518492B2).

     Distinct.  Listed for completeness.


4. CONCERNING PRIOR ART - BRIEF 2 (branching arrangement)
---------------------------------------------------------

4.1  Senode (iOS application, ~2017) and MIDINOUS (desktop
     application, 2024).  *Most concerning category.*

     Both are step-sequencers explicitly modelled as a directed
     graph of musical nodes connected by edges, with run-time
     traversal.  This overlaps the headline "graph not list"
     claim.

     The web documentation for both is shallow.  Per what I could
     find, neither selects edges by external performer pitch
     class - Senode appears self-driving, MIDINOUS selects edges
     by random / immediate / path-creation-order - but I cannot
     rule out an undocumented feature.

     What I want from you:  obtain the full product documenta-
     tion (including manuals, marketing copy, and any developer
     blogs or interviews) for both products and confirm whether
     either supports a per-node branch table indexed by external
     performer keystroke pitch class.  This is the single most
     important diligence item for Brief 2.

4.2  LucasArts iMUSE (US5315057 and continuations, 1994).

     The canonical interactive music branching system.  Branches
     are decided at composer-authored decision points based on
     game-state events, not on the pitch of a performer's
     keystroke.

     What I want from you:  pull the full LucasArts /
     Lucasfilm patent family from that period.  Although the
     trigger mechanism is distinct (game-state vs. pitch-class),
     the broader "graph of musical segments with run-time
     branching" structure is firmly established prior art and
     I expect this to be the principal citation in any
     examination.

4.3  Audiokinetic Wwise / Firelight FMOD interactive-music
     engines (current commercial products with various filings).

     Same family as iMUSE; game-state-parameter-driven
     branching.  Distinct from pitch-class-keyed performer
     trigger.  Standard search target.

4.4  Yamaha PSR / Genos / Tyros style section change (MAIN A/B/
     C/D + FILL + INTRO + ENDING).

     Performer-driven section change in real time, but via
     dedicated control buttons on the instrument, not via the
     pitch class of a played note.  Yamaha hold a substantial
     patent family in this area; please pull it.

     This is the closest "performer-driven real-time section
     change" prior art I am aware of.  The distinction rests on:
     (a) selection by pitch class of a played note rather than
     by dedicated section button, (b) per-pattern branch table
     of up to 12 entries rather than a fixed global set of
     section variations, and (c) integration with the per-
     pattern performer-sync engine of Brief 1.

4.5  Tracker pattern-jump / pattern-break effects (MOD/XM/IT
     formats: Bxx, Dxx).

     Composer-authored, deterministic.  Distinct.
